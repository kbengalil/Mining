## Red Flags
*   **Valuation: Government Take and State Carried Interests:** While after-tax NPV5% is disclosed for the Cariboo project, and royalties are included in operating costs, the documents do not explicitly mention whether any host government has a "carried interest" (an equity stake given to the government at no cost, which reduces investor-level NPV) that has been netted out of the NPV calculation. Rick Rule highlights that this distinction is frequently missed and can significantly reduce the true investor-level NPV.
*   **Dilution: Private Placements Signal Management's View:** The company has completed multiple private placements and public offerings between August 2025 and June 2026, including US$300 million in Convertible Senior Notes. As per Rick Rule's framework, when a company issues shares or debt via private placement, management explicitly signals that cash is more valuable to them than a piece of the business at that moment. The investor must verify if the use of this capital justifies the dilution. The stated purpose for the Convertible Notes was to "advance the Cariboo Gold Project, supporting continued development activities and accelerating momentum toward a final investment decision."
*   **Dilution: Growing Share Count:** The basic shares outstanding have increased from 118.95 million as of December 31, 2020, to 305.8 million as of July 3, 2026, indicating significant dilution of existing shareholders.
*   **Dilution: Outstanding Warrants and Options:** There are 116.7 million warrants and 10.1 million options, DSUs, and RSUs outstanding (as of July 3, 2026), which represent future potential dilution.
*   **Legal & Environmental: Indigenous Agreements in Progress:** For the Cariboo Gold Project, the company is "working towards an agreement with the Xatśūll First Nation." While "strong support" from Indigenous nations is cited generally, the ongoing negotiation with a specific First Nation indicates that final agreements are not yet universal across all relevant groups.
*   **Legal & Environmental: Environmental Rehabilitation Provision:** The company has an environmental rehabilitation provision of $53.236 million as of December 31, 2021, and has estimated LOM reclamation and closure costs of C$135 million for the Cariboo project. These are substantial future obligations.
*   **Financials: Burn Rate Not Recently Disclosed:** The most recent detailed financial statements are for December 31, 2021. For later periods, while cash balance is provided (March 31, 2025), a current burn rate is not disclosed in the provided investor documents, making it difficult to assess the cash runway without making assumptions.
*   **Jurisdiction: Sovereign Risk in Mexico:** The San Antonio Gold Project is located in Sonora, Mexico. The 2021 MD&A highlights increased criminal activity and violence in parts of Mexico, impacting the mining sector through kidnapping, extortion, and armed robberies of operations and supply convoys. This presents a sovereign risk factor affecting operations in that jurisdiction.
*   **Business Stage: No Commercial Production to Date:** The company has not achieved full commercial production from any of its projects. Small-scale heap leaching and test mining at Tintic in 2025 and at Bonanza Ledge II in 2021 were undertaken. The Bonanza Ledge II project was impaired to $nil recoverable amount as of September 30, 2021. This means the company has not yet demonstrated consistent profitable operations from mining.
*   **Business Stage: Dependence on External Financing:** The company's ability to develop projects and achieve production is "highly dependent upon its ability to raise equity and debt capital in the financial markets." This implies a continuous need for financing, which can lead to further dilution of existing shareholders.
*   **Business Stage: Existing Streaming and Royalty Agreements:**
    *   **Cariboo Gold Project & Bonanza Ledge II Project:** Osisko Gold Royalties holds a 5% Net Smelter Return (NSR) royalty.
    *   **San Antonio Gold Project:** Osisko Gold Royalties (through Osisko Bermuda Ltd) holds a 15% gold and silver stream, requiring payment of 15% of the daily market price for payable metals.
    *   **James Bay & Guerrero Exploration Properties:** Osisko Gold Royalties holds a 3.0% NSR royalty.
    *   **Tintic Project:** A non-binding stream term sheet with Osisko Gold Royalties (Osisko Bermuda Ltd) for US$20-40 million upfront. If US$40 million is drawn, 5% of all metals produced up to 53,400 ounces of refined gold, then 4.0% thereafter. The company receives 25% of spot prices for delivered metal. These agreements reduce the company's share of future revenue from these projects.
